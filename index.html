Akpevweoghene Ogheneyoma is a communication researcher. She has been enthusiastic about communication since her secondary school days when she found the magnificent world of literary fiction and its power to communicate feelings, gestures and other life occurences she could not previously explain. Deciding to study communication after this experience, she devotes her life to the study and research of communication patterns, hoping to unravel the unique process behind communication and help people become better recipients of messages as well as better communicators themselves.

Her research interests lies in strategic business and political communication, particularly those that promote sustainable developments. She has a bachelor's degree in mass communication from the prestigious Delta State University, Abraka, Nigeria (DELSU). Her research work on the effects of online advertising of the iPhone on undergraduate students of DELSU is currently under publication. She aims start her master's degree in communication by this September, 2025.

She currently works as a teacher and corporate communication strategist at Akpevweoghene Educational Centre, Nigeria (AEC) and has been in this role since 2022. Prior to this, she worked as a programmer at Delta Broadcasting Service, Warri for six months during her university internship program. She also volunteered as a teaching assistant in AEC during all the summer holidays beginning from her sophomore year at university until she graduated in 2022 and delved into full-time teaching. Owing to this, she has a significant amount of experience in teaching and in mass communication to a large audience.
